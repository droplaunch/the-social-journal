Graduates 6:16 and Testing_Grounds.mp3 — Behind the Beats

Published on: April 12, 2025
Author: Gokul Sanjeev Shenoy

The Vision

Graduates 6:16 ain’t just music — it’s a statement.

A sonic journal of ambition, heartbreak, ego, pressure, freedom, rebellion — everything I’ve lived through while building my legacy. Inspired by the likes of Kanye’s Donda, Life of Pablo, and the rawness of underground sounds, the project blends soul samples, trap drums, spoken word, and even voice note glitches.

The Sound

  • “On Read” — A follow-up to “Homewrecker”, this track spirals through regret and resilience over a Travis Scott inspired beat.
  • “MetroLife” — Beat built entirely on subway noise and 808s. It’s claustrophobic but slick.
  • “NDA Freestyle” — Just me, the mic, and truths I can’t say on record. Inspired by late-night freestyle energy.
  • “Homewrecker” — Gospel vocal chops meet the rage sound. A breakup letter disguised as a love song.

Process & Inspiration

I built this album in between study sessions, breakdowns, ComputeX lessons, and working on my SATs.
The irony? This might outlive the unreleased projects I planned.

Tools:
• SoundTrap + Voice Recorder + Voloco
• Kanye’s stem player for experimenting
• Random movie dialogues, late-night TED talks, and voice memos from homies

Why Now?

I’m graduating soon, and this is my way of saying goodbye to the past while embracing the future. It’s a digital time capsule of my journey, a soundtrack to the chaos and clarity of these years.

I’m not just a student anymore. I’m a creator, a hustler, and this album is my graduation — from high school, from the old me.
