Graduates 6:16 — Behind the Beats

Published on: April 12, 2025
Author: Gokul / gpmafia

The Vision

Graduates 6:16 ain’t just music — it’s a statement.

A sonic journal of ambition, heartbreak, ego, pressure, freedom, rebellion — everything I’ve lived through while building my legacy. Inspired by the likes of Kanye’s Donda, Life of Pablo, and the rawness of underground sounds, the project blends soul samples, trap drums, spoken word, and even voice note glitches.

The Sound

  • “On Read” — A follow-up to Isabella, this track spirals through regret and resilience over a dusty Smokey Robinson loop.
  • “MetroLife” — Beat built entirely on subway noise and 808s. It’s claustrophobic but slick.
  • “NDA Freestyle” — Just me, the mic, and truths I can’t say on record. Inspired by late-night freestyle energy and Gadzhi monologues.
  • “You Lied About Forever” — Gospel vocal chops meet cold synths. A breakup letter disguised as a love song.

Process & Inspiration

I built this album in between study sessions, breakdowns, ComputeX lessons, and working on my SATs.
The irony? This might outlive my grades.

Tools:
• FL Studio + analog tape saturation
• Kanye’s stem player workflow for experimenting
• Random movie dialogues, late-night TED talks, and voice memos from homies

Style

• All black.
• Typeface: Courier New.
• Visuals: Inspired by old TV boot screens, hacker forums, and VHS distortion.
• Themes: Rebellion, Ambition, Legacy, and the Digital Self.
