How I Managed Internships in High School

People think high school is just classes and exams. For me, it was also about building my lane — and that meant internships.

I started off raw. I had no connections, no resume — just skills and the drive to build something real. My first internship was in AI/ML, and I had to learn how to balance code with school deadlines and burnout.

So how did I manage?

  • 🧠 Strict scheduling: I planned weekends like a CEO. 2 hours for research, 3 hours for tasks, and gym before bed.
  • 💼 Cold emailing: I didn’t wait for offers. I pitched myself like I was already the solution.
  • 📊 Portfolio power: I made pages that *looked* clean AF — even if the backend was still under construction (like this one 😉).
  • 🤖 Built value: Whether it was ComputeX or project managing small AI tasks, I always gave more than I got.

Now I use these same skills for every side quest — from music production to tinkering with bots. Internships gave me my blueprint, but it was how I managed them that defined me.
